# Navigator Playbook: Retinopathy and SDOH v1

**Status:** L1 operational draft.  
**Audience:** Community health workers, navigators, care coordinators, navigator supervisors.  
**Boundary:** Navigators do not diagnose, change treatment, or close clinical concerns without source evidence or clinical review.

## Navigator Role

The navigator is the first customer of returned information. Sandy should handle protocol-safe explanation, reminders, barrier collection, and resource matching. The navigator handles exceptions, uncertainty, logistics, community-resource connection, and human trust.

## Queue Handling Principles

1. Read the queue reason first.
2. Check the protocol trail.
3. Check source facts and patient statements.
4. Confirm the next safe action.
5. Document the action taken.
6. Close the queue item only when the work is actually resolved or handed off.

## Standard Queue Workflow

1. Open the queue item.
2. Verify patient identity according to program policy.
3. Review why Sandy created the item.
4. Review the suggested action.
5. Contact patient or partner as appropriate.
6. Update resource, appointment, or reconciliation status.
7. Mark done only after the next step is confirmed.

## Queue Reason Playbooks

### Transportation Barrier

Goal: Get the patient to the screening option without promising a ride before confirmation.

Steps:

- Confirm appointment/site preference.
- Confirm pickup location and timing needs.
- Check available ride support from site, MCO benefit, LKLP/public transit, family/caregiver, or community partner.
- Document ride source and status.
- If no ride is available, route to alternate screening site or scheduling window.

Suggested patient language:

> "I see you told Sandy transportation may get in the way. I can help check which ride options are actually available before we ask you to travel."

### Cost Barrier

Goal: Clarify likely coverage or low-cost options without giving a benefits guarantee.

Steps:

- Check insurance/coverage hints if available.
- Identify low-cost or FQHC screening option.
- Contact site/payer pathway if needed.
- Tell patient only what has been confirmed.
- Document unresolved coverage uncertainty.

### After-Hours Needed

Goal: Match the patient to a practical appointment window.

Steps:

- Confirm work/caregiver constraints.
- Check weekend/evening/mobile screening options.
- Offer the next confirmed feasible option.
- If none exist, keep navigator task open and set follow-up.

### Patient Not Ready

Goal: Preserve trust and keep the door open.

Steps:

- Ask what is making the patient hesitate.
- Use approved education, not pressure.
- Offer a future reminder or navigator callback.
- Document preference and reason.

### Already Completed Needs Reconciliation

Goal: Avoid false gap closure.

Steps:

- Ask where and when screening occurred.
- Request permission to verify if needed.
- Check claims/HIE/site feed/manual record.
- Close only when source evidence or navigator-reviewed documentation supports it.

### SDOH Resource Connection

Goal: Help the patient connect to a relevant community resource without promising eligibility.

Steps:

- Confirm need type and urgency.
- Review resource source and verified date.
- Confirm whether the resource is kynect/211, KY Partner, local county guide, or manually curated.
- If kynect partner access exists, follow approved referral workflow.
- If no live referral access exists, contact resource or 211 and document availability.
- Ask patient permission before sharing contact details outside the app.

Suggested patient language:

> "Sandy found a possible resource. I am going to confirm it is open, relevant, and available before we call this connected."

### Red Flag Symptom

Goal: Stop routine navigation and route to urgent clinical guidance.

Steps:

- Treat as urgent.
- Do not reassure.
- Use approved urgent guidance script.
- Notify clinical escalation path.
- Document contact attempt and outcome.
- Keep routine Sandy flow locked until reviewed.

### Abnormal Result Referral

Goal: Ensure abnormal screening result leads to appropriate follow-up.

Steps:

- Confirm result source and date.
- Confirm referral destination.
- Help patient understand that follow-up is needed without interpreting the result beyond approved copy.
- Schedule or confirm referral support.

### Ungradable Repeat Needed

Goal: Arrange repeat imaging.

Steps:

- Explain that the image could not be read well enough.
- Match repeat site.
- Address barriers.
- Confirm plan.

## Escalation Rules

Escalate to clinical reviewer when:

- Patient reports acute/red-flag symptoms.
- Patient asks for diagnosis or treatment changes.
- Result is abnormal and referral path is unclear.
- Imported data conflicts and could affect care.
- Identity match is uncertain.

Escalate to supervisor when:

- Resource partner cannot be reached after two attempts.
- Patient cannot be contacted after program-defined attempts.
- Patient requests complaint, privacy concern, or opt-out.

## Documentation Minimums

Each navigator action should document:

- Contact attempt and channel.
- Patient-stated need or barrier.
- Source reviewed.
- Resource/site contacted if applicable.
- Availability/eligibility status if confirmed.
- Next step and owner.
- Whether patient consent was needed and obtained.

## Tabletop Scenarios

Before pilot, the navigator team should rehearse:

1. Patient needs a ride to Saturday screening.
2. Patient reports screening already done elsewhere.
3. Patient says they suddenly lost vision.
4. Patient asks Sandy whether to change diabetes medication.
5. Patient requests food help through Kentucky resources.
6. Patient gets abnormal result requiring referral.
7. Patient has ungradable image requiring repeat screening.
